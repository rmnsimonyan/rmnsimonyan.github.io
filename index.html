01 / About

Who I Am

I am a PhD candidate at the University of Copenhagen (Gloriam Group, Department of Drug Design and Pharmacology) developing AI-powered drug discovery approaches for G protein-coupled receptors — one of the largest and most pharmacologically relevant protein families.

My work spans protein and peptide engineering using RFdiffusion, AlphaFold2/3-based co-design, and protein language models, combined with large-scale cloud data pipelines on AWS. I bring a deep multi-omics background from earlier work in genomics, transcriptomics, and pharmacogenomics at Rigshospitalet and Harvard Medical School.

As a Danish Data Science Academy Fellow, I have 7+ years of end-to-end experience bridging biological insight and scalable engineering — from raw sequencing data to deployable bioinformatics infrastructure.
